Mean Reversion Research Basis

SENTINEL's methodology is grounded in published quantitative research. Below are the key works and concepts that inform the system.

Published Works

  • Larry Connors & Cesar Alvarez
    "Short Term Trading Strategies That Work"

    Extensive backtests of RSI(2) as a mean reversion indicator across equity markets. Demonstrated that extreme RSI(2) readings (<10 and >90) have historically preceded short-term reversals with statistical significance. SENTINEL adapts this approach to cryptocurrency markets.

  • Ernest P. Chan
    "Quantitative Trading" and "Algorithmic Trading"

    Comprehensive frameworks for implementing statistical arbitrage and mean reversion strategies. Covers position sizing, risk management, and the mathematics of mean-reverting time series. Key source for SENTINEL's risk framework and portfolio construction methodology.

  • Colin Bennett
    "Trading Volatility, Correlation, Term Structure and Skew"

    Volatility-regime analysis for entry and exit timing. Bennett's work on realized vs. implied volatility dynamics informs SENTINEL's understanding of when mean reversion conditions are most likely to succeed.

Key Statistical Concepts

Concept
RSI(2) — Short-Period RSI

The Relative Strength Index with a 2-period lookback captures extreme short-term momentum shifts. Unlike RSI(14), RSI(2) oscillates rapidly between extremes, making it ideal for identifying capitulation and exhaustion events.

Concept
Bollinger Bands — 2σ Deviation

Bollinger Bands (20-period, 2 standard deviations) provide a dynamic envelope around price. Penetration of the outer bands represents statistically rare events — approximately 5% of all candles touch or exceed 2σ, making these high-probability reversion zones.

Concept
Volume Spike Confirmation

Above-average volume during price extremes confirms that the move is driven by capitulation (panic selling) or exhaustion (euphoric buying). These high-volume extreme events historically precede mean reversion more reliably than low-volume drifts.

The application of these methods to cryptocurrency markets is SENTINEL's own adaptation. Academic results in equity or fixed-income markets do not transfer directly to crypto markets, which exhibit different microstructure, liquidity profiles, and volatility characteristics.
