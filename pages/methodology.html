Statistical Mean Reversion Explained

A complete breakdown of SENTINEL's entry logic, risk framework, and the statistical principles behind every signal.

What is Mean Reversion?

Mean reversion is a well-documented statistical phenomenon: prices that deviate significantly from their historical average tend to return toward that average over time. This happens in crypto markets with striking regularity — not because markets are rational, but because extreme moves trigger counter-reactions.


Oversold assets attract buyers. Overbought assets attract sellers. SENTINEL's job is to identify these extremes with mathematical precision — and enter counter-trend positions at statistically advantageous moments.


This is not intuition. This is measurement.

The Three Conditions

Every SENTINEL signal requires all three conditions to be true simultaneously.

Condition 01
RSI(2) Extreme

RSI(2) below 10 for buy signals. RSI(2) above 90 for sell signals. RSI with period 2 reacts to short-term extremes that RSI(14) misses entirely. Backtested extensively by Larry Connors. Edge confirmed in academic literature.

Condition 02
Bollinger Band Penetration

Price must touch or cross below the lower Bollinger Band (20-period, 2σ). This confirms that the price deviation exceeds 2 standard deviations from the mean — a statistically rare event (~5% of all candles).

Condition 03
Volume Confirmation

Volume must exceed the 20-period average by at least 50%. High volume on extreme moves confirms capitulation or exhaustion — the selling/buying pressure that precedes the reversion.

ALL THREE MUST BE TRUE SIMULTANEOUSLY. If any condition is absent, no signal fires.

Risk Framework

Mean reversion is a powerful edge — but it comes with a specific risk: during strong trending markets, reversion signals fail repeatedly. SENTINEL manages this with three layers:

  • 1

    Position size: 1% maximum risk per trade

    Small enough to survive a streak of consecutive losses without significant drawdown.

  • 2

    Hard stop: 2% maximum loss per position

    No exceptions. Every position has a predefined exit point.

  • 3

    Time exit: positions closed after 5 days

    Waiting for mean reversion in a trending market can result in large losses. SENTINEL cuts the position after 5 days and reassesses — preserving capital for the next statistically valid opportunity.
